## Why this matters for the [[references/elevenlabs-growth-generalist-jd|Growth Generalist]] application

1. **The hiring filter is engineer-operator, not marketer-pedigree.** Luke himself is Microsoft Research → YC founder → PostHog → ElevenLabs. He has never held a "Marketing Manager" title. He hires for the same shape: builder + commercial intuition + writing + analytical, not pedigreed CMO-track.
2. **His "Your first growth hire" essay is the closest public spec for the seat.** Re-read it before applying. The framing of "generalists vs. specialists" is the JD lineage.
3. **The "10x engineers talk to users" essay** is his version of the [[concepts/founding-gtm-engineer-thesis|engineer-seller thesis]]. Mark's framing should mirror Luke's vocabulary, not the standard "demand-gen / pipeline-first / RevOps" marketer lexicon.
4. **The "$100K saved with AI tooling" podcast** establishes that AI-first is a measurable bar, not just culture-deck language. [[skills/ai-powered-sales-tooling]] is the asset Mark should lead with.
5. **PostHog lineage**: PostHog is open-source, developer-first, product-led-growth. Luke's mental model for "growth" is closer to PLG / community / product-led than to enterprise demand-gen. Frame Stackgini's intra-IT motion accordingly.

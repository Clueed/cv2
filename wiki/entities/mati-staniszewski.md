# Mati Staniszewski

> Co-founder and CEO of ElevenLabs. Polish background, London-based. Architect of the public operating model (no titles, AI-first, multiples-of-11). His public statements on company direction are the strongest signal for where the next 2-3 years of GTM hiring sits.

## Operating principles (in his voice)

From public talks + the officechai no-titles essay + Summit keynotes:

- **Eliminate job titles, organise around impact.** Company runs as **~20 micro-teams of 5-10 people**. Rule: "no task is above or beneath you."
- **AI-first across all functions.** Engineering, growth, and operations all leverage AI; not as a culture-deck claim but as a measurable bar (Luke Harries cites $100K+ saved on AI tooling).
- **Customer-service TAM is the wedge.** Summit Feb 2026 keynote: "$400B/yr on customer service globally; average UK resident spends 10 hours on hold/year." See [[concepts/voice-ai-enterprise-tam]].
- **"Multiples of 11, always"** — JD body explicitly enshrines this internal numerology around the company's name; appears in revenue/headcount/comms framing.
- **Polish-rooted, globally distributed.** Considers Warsaw Stock Exchange dual listing partly to "create and give back something" to the Polish innovation ecosystem.

## Why this matters for the [[references/elevenlabs-growth-generalist-jd|Growth Generalist]] application

Mati is **not the Growth Generalist hiring manager** ([[entities/luke-harries|Luke Harries]] is), but his public framing sets the operating ceiling for the seat:

1. **The IPO timeline (2-3 years) and the $11B valuation** mean the seat is being hired into the **pre-IPO commercial scale-up phase**, not the seed/Series-A founding-marketer phase. Operator-shape requirements (multi-project ownership, pipeline math, infrastructure-grade reporting) are non-negotiable.
2. **The Polish-Warsaw dimension** matters culturally: the company has hubs in Warsaw and London, founder identity is Polish, and the "give back to Poland" framing is real. Not directly Vienna-relevant but signals the **CEE-friendly remote stance** that makes Vienna more plausible than (say) a SF-only employer would.
3. **The "multiples of 11" + no-titles culture** is **performative discipline, not just whimsy** — applicants who treat these as quirky-marketing-deck-language will misread the room.

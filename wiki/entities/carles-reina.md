# Carles Reina

> VP Revenue / Global Revenue Strategy at ElevenLabs. Not the Growth Generalist seat's direct manager (that's [[entities/luke-harries|Luke Harries]] on the growth/marketing wing), but the load-bearing figure on the sales/revenue wing that the marketing org feeds.

## Current role

**VP Revenue, ElevenLabs.** First GTM hire. Employee #4. Also was the **first ElevenLabs investor** before joining as employee. Scaled the GTM function **from $0 → $330M ARR in 3 years** (Jan 2023 to early 2026), then to **$500M+ ARR by May 2026**.

**International scope**: launched ElevenLabs Enterprise across **US, Europe, Japan, India, Korea, Brazil, Mexico, Colombia, and the Middle East**.

## GTM philosophy

From the Carles Reina ElevenLabs playbook ([Product Market Fit podcast](https://www.productmarketfit.tech/p/carles-reina-300m-arr-elevenlabs), Harry Stebbings's "8 lessons from Carles Reina on scaling $330M revenue at ElevenLabs" LinkedIn distillation, and the Akash Bajwa newsletter):

- **High-output per head** — small teams, aggressive productivity expectations, systems that turn revenue expansion into a repeatable machine.
- **20x quota rule** — every quota-carrying hire must bring in **20x their base salary in revenue**. If your base is $100K, your annual quota is $2M. The rule functions as **a culture filter, not just a comp mechanic** — it screens for sales hires who believe they can deliver that multiple.
- **Pessimistic forecasting** — forecast conservatively, beat the number.
- **Expansion crediting** — both AE and CSM get paid on upsells (alignment design).
- **Operating cadence** — monthly pipeline reviews, weekly outbound discipline.
- **Six sales-org primitives** he frames everything around: segments, roles, quota, OTE, commission mechanics, crediting rules.

## Adjacent activities

Carles also runs a **€12.9M solo GP fund** investing in early-stage European startups in three sectors (per Silicon Canals coverage). The dual operator-investor identity is a recurring shape inside ElevenLabs senior staff (he himself was the first ElevenLabs investor before joining).

# Stackgini

B2B SaaS for **agentic enterprise IT decision-making** — i.e., the product helps large enterprises run multi-stakeholder IT procurement and vendor selection cycles. Founded with [[entities/mark-vavulov|Mark Vavulov]] as the first non-founder hire (Nov 2023).

## Company state (as of 2026-05)

- ~10 full-time employees, started bootstrapped in a co-working space
- **VC-backed.** Pre-seed raised 2024; seed round closed with international investors (closed by 2026-05). ^[inferred — Mark stated the seed "just closed" in the 2026-05-31 conversation]
- ARR: just over $1M; was ~$20K with 1 customer when Mark joined Nov 2023 (per Mark 2026-06-09; earlier wiki estimate of $50K was wrong)
- 35 total customers today (per Mark 2026-06-09); 25 enterprise accounts closed personally by Mark at 30% win rate, $50K ACV, ~6-month average sales cycle
- Geography: primarily DACH, significant European presence, first US customer (Danaher) closed in 2025 via SAP LeanIX partnership

## The product

Human-in-the-loop AI workflows for enterprise IT decisions. Mark personally ran ~10 one-hour workflow reviews per week and maintained delivery SLAs. ^[inferred] (the product implication — "agentic enterprise IT decision-making" with HITL — is the user's own phrasing across multiple sessions)

## Buying center — the intra-IT framing

**"Multi-stakeholder enterprise sales cycle" at Stackgini means alignment across IT sub-functions, NOT across IT / finance / legal / procurement.**

The actual stakeholder groups in a Stackgini buying center:

- Enterprise architecture
- IT procurement
- InfoSec
- IT business partnering
- IT governance
- Software asset management (ITAM)

**Why this matters:** Stackgini sells to IT decision-makers. The buying center sits entirely inside IT. Defaulting to the generic "IT, finance, procurement, legal" formulation would confuse anyone in a buyer organization reading the CV or pitch.

**How to apply** (for CV tailoring or any pitch material): describe multi-stakeholder cycles using the IT sub-function language above. Never default to the generic cross-functional formulation. See the memory file `feedback_stackgini_buying_centers.md`.

## CS → Professional Services pivot

Initial customer success function was tried as a classic CS model. The enterprise customer profile (very large, complex process tools) demanded more — the team moved CS to a **professional-services / consulting-style engagement model** rather than ticket-driven CS. Mark contributed to this decision but did not own it; he was involved in hiring the CS function but not as owner.

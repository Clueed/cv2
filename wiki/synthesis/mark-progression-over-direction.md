# Mark — Job-Search Filter: Progression Over Direction

## Context

The wiki's [[synthesis/mark-job-direction-map]] organizes role-shape options around the [[concepts/founding-gtm-engineer-thesis]] — engineer-seller as the load-bearing narrative. A 2026-05-30 conversation about a specific Vienna IR role surfaced that the engineer-seller thesis was being misused as a **direction filter** when it was only ever a **description of current leverage**. The map's own *Gaps* section called this out as missing: "dealbreakers", "vertical preferences beyond AI", "leadership ambition." This page fills that gap.

## Finding

Mark's primary decision filter for jobs is **career progression** (scope, ownership, signal, comp trajectory), not a particular domain.

- He has no special affinity for GTM / AE / commercial work — it is his current core expertise at [[entities/stackgini]], not a calling.
- For the **next role transition**, leveraging that expertise (commercial-adjacent or generalized-GTM) is the pragmatic move because domain depth is the strongest lever he currently has.
- **Longer-term direction is open** and decided per-opportunity, not predetermined. He is "very open to other opportunities" in general but expects to figure out direction on an individual-job basis. ^[inferred]

## Reasoning

Direct statement from Mark (2026-05-30):

> "I don't really care for [GTM] that much, but this is currently where I have the most expertise... staying on the commercial side is probably better for my next step in terms of having domain expertise... I'm most interested in just progressing in my career and am very career-focused. I want to progress in which direction, and I don't really care. I'll figure it out on an individual basis for each job."

What changed for the wiki:

- The [[concepts/founding-gtm-engineer-thesis]] remains an accurate description of where Mark's *current leverage* sits. Misuse to date was treating it as Mark's *identity preference*.
- [[synthesis/mark-job-direction-map]]'s Tier ordering and shortlist suggestions implicitly assumed he wanted to *remain* an engineer-seller. That weighting is incorrect; the enumeration itself is fine.

## Implications

- **Next-step shortlist should bias toward commercial-adjacent or generalized-GTM seats** to compound existing expertise — pragmatic, not identity-driven.
- **Don't filter out off-commercial roles** (engineering, investing, finance, founding), but don't auto-rank them above commercial-adjacent for the *next* step.
- **Per-role evaluation**: for any specific opportunity, judge on progression terms (scope, ownership, signal, comp trajectory) rather than thesis fit.
- **Hybrid roles** (mixed commercial + off-commercial) are fine when the off-commercial fraction adds progression — they do not have to be "the pivot."
- **Do not extrapolate direction preferences from single statements.** Earlier in the same conversation, Mark said "I don't care about GTM/EA" — interpreted in isolation as "ditch GTM," which over-corrected. Corrected reading: domain isn't the filter; progression is. ^[inferred]

### Worked example: Allocator One Group, Investment Manager, Vienna

JD discussed in source conversation. Splits roughly:

- **~70% commercial** — IR / fundraising / LP communications / data-room ownership. Different audience (capital-side) but same muscle (selling).
- **~30% off-commercial** — VC/PE/private-credit fund modeling (waterfalls, IRR, carry); AI agent workflows on Infra One (their AI-native fund operating platform).

Under the corrected filter:

- It is **not "a pivot away from commercial"** — it is a different commercial domain (selling to LPs instead of enterprise IT buyers).
- Progression case: gives capital-side network, fund-economics fluency, and AI-engineering shipped on a production platform — none of which exist in a pure SaaS-AE next step.
- Likelihood of landing: **low (~10–15% to interview, ~3–5% to offer)** ^[inferred] — no fund-modeling reps, no private-markets background, competing against candidates who do.
- Decision under corrected filter: worth a speculative pitch only if Vienna geography + capital-network + AI-eng artifacts outweigh the commercial-AE alternatives already on `jobs/vienna-targets.md`. The strongest applicant angle is the AI-native-operator framing ([[skills/ai-powered-sales-tooling]]), not the investment-professional framing.

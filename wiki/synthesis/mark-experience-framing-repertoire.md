# Stackgini Experience × Application Framing Repertoire

## The Connection

Six application packages exist in the wiki — [[references/anthropic-final-resume-2026-05|Anthropic]], [[references/allocator-one-application-2026-05-31|Allocator One]], [[references/vendure-application-2026-06-03|Vendure]], [[references/elevenlabs-application-2026-06-13|ElevenLabs]], [[references/enpulsion-application-2026-06-15|ENPULSION]], [[references/fonio-application-2026-06-15|fonio]]. Each is a frozen tailoring of the **same underlying fact base**, bent toward one role shape. Read individually they are application snapshots. Read together they are two things no single page captures:

1. **The complete canonical inventory** of what [[entities/mark-vavulov|Mark]] actually did — larger than any one CV shows, because every CV prunes to fit one page and one audience.
2. **A reusable framing library** — each experience atom has 2–5 battle-tested reframings keyed to role shape, plus a hard line of what is *never* claimed.

This page is the lookup table. Per-application tailoring stops being a from-scratch exercise and becomes: pick the role shape → pull the matching framings → check the "never claim" line.

## Where They Co-occur

Every snapshot draws from the same atoms — scale claim, AI tooling, enterprise sales, ops cadence, marketing/content, partner channel, CS pivot, fundraising support, exec-audience deck-craft, category creation, cross-functional generalist range. What changes across snapshots is *which atoms appear*, *how many bullets*, *which framing of each atom*, and *the interests line*. The [[skills/ai-powered-sales-tooling]] page holds the raw tooling facts; [[entities/mark-vavulov]] holds the operating profile and fundraising detail; the snapshots hold the proven phrasings.

## Cross-cutting Insight

The factual base is **fixed and finite** — Part 1 is the whole of it. What varies across every application is *selection and framing*, not substance. That means the high-leverage move is never inventing new claims; it is (a) selecting the 5–7 atoms that match the JD's pillars, (b) pulling the framing variant that mirrors the JD's own vocabulary, and (c) holding the "never claim" lines. The repertoire in Part 3 is now a lookup, not a re-derivation.

Three atoms do the heaviest differentiating work and recur in almost every package: **scale + category creation** (no other candidate from a consulting/MBA track has a comparable $20K→$1M founding claim), **the three AI tools** (the engineer-seller proof, unique vs typical GTM applicants), and **fundraising support** (the only transferable story for capital-side seats). Everything else is situational ballast.

The framing also reveals a consistent *voice spine* independent of role: information-dense and dry, KPIs frontloaded, no em dashes ([[feedback_no_em_dashes]]), no self-hedging ([[feedback_no_self_hedging]]), claims stated directly, and a hard discipline about what is *not* claimed (no waterfalls/LP-side per [[feedback_finance_claims_scope]]; no invented domain expertise in space-tech or voice-AI; no title rebrand).

## Tensions and Trade-offs

- **$50K vs $20K ARR.** The verified starting figure is $20K (1 customer), used from ElevenLabs onward. Anthropic, Allocator One, Vendure, and ENPULSION snapshots — and **both [[entities/stackgini]] and [[entities/mark-vavulov]]** — still carry $50K. The entity pages should be corrected so the canonical base is unambiguous.
- **ICP database vs "Clay/Apollo equivalent."** Through ENPULSION, the ABM layer was framed as an "in-house Clay/Apollo equivalent." fonio retired that for the architecture-accurate "Europe-wide ICP scoring database sourcing signals off-the-shelf vendors don't provide." The newer framing is canonical (see [[project_stackgini_gtm_stack]]); the older one lingers in earlier snapshots and the [[references/fonio-gtm-engineer-jd]] analysis page.
- **Specificity vs page budget.** Vendure restored seven bullets to cover six PMM pillars; Allocator One collapsed to three. More atoms per page means each gets less weight — the fonio CV already overflowed to 2 pages and needs a trim. Comprehensiveness and the one-page constraint pull against each other.
- **Commercial bias vs progression filter.** The repertoire is overwhelmingly commercial, which risks reinforcing a GTM identity Mark doesn't hold — per [[synthesis/mark-progression-over-direction]], he filters by progression, not domain. The ENPULSION (CoS) and Allocator One (IR) reframings are the proof that the same fact base flexes off-commercial; the repertoire should be read as range, not as a commercial pigeonhole.
- **Productization signal, named vs unnamed.** [[user_productization_affinity]] is load-bearing in the Allocator One opener and ENPULSION bullet 3, but is deliberately never labeled "productization" in copy — it's carried by example. Naming it would flatten it.

## Open Questions

- **Canonical fact-base page.** Should Part 1 become the single source of truth that every future snapshot diffs against, with the entity pages reduced to pointers? Today the facts are smeared across the entity page, the skills page, and six snapshots.
- **Cover letters for ENPULSION and fonio** remain undrafted; both need a why-company / why-domain answer the CV can't carry.
- **Unframed atoms.** Freelance merchandising and the IT/ISO-27001 track at Luther are rarely surfaced — is there a role shape (field execution, IT-GRC-adjacent) where they become load-bearing rather than ballast?
- **Interests as signal vs noise.** The interests line has become a tuning surface; at what point does heavy per-role recomposition read as over-fitting to a reviewer who sees the pattern?

# Mark — Broad Job Direction Map

A deliberately wide enumeration of role shapes that fit [[entities/mark-vavulov|Mark]]'s profile. Not a shortlist or ranking — the goal is breadth, so that any subsequent narrowing has the full search space visible. Filters that would compress this list (compensation floor, geography, leadership ambition, role dealbreakers) are not yet captured in the wiki — see **Gaps** below.

## Context

Mark's profile sits at an unusual intersection: ~2.5 years as founding GTM operator at [[entities/stackgini|Stackgini]] (sub-$50K → $1M+ ARR), three shipped AI sales systems built on raw LLM APIs ([[skills/ai-powered-sales-tooling]]), strong anti-lowcode stance, quantitative business background plus paused CS studies ([[references/mark-education]]), and enterprise-deal reps with F500 anchors (DHL, Siemens Healthineers, Danaher, REWE, HAYS, National Bank of Liechtenstein). He is currently chasing stretch roles at frontier labs ([[references/anthropic-gtm-strategy-ops-dach-jd]]).

The [[concepts/founding-gtm-engineer-thesis]] is the load-bearing narrative: an engineer-seller in a founding GTM seat compounds because they build leverage rather than buy or hire for it. The role space below is organized around where that thesis remains a hiring advantage. ^[inferred]

## Reasoning

The typology is derived from three filters applied to Mark's profile: ^[inferred]

1. **Where engineering leverage is priced into the role** — categories 1, 2, 3, 7. These are the natural home of the engineer-seller and the cleanest match for the [[concepts/founding-gtm-engineer-thesis|thesis]].
2. **Where customer-facing depth at the enterprise level is priced in** — categories 1, 4, 5, 8. F500 reps are the rarer half of Mark's profile and unlock incumbents and growth-stage roles.
3. **Where personal interests sit independent of professional fit** — category 9. Included for completeness even when the market match is thinner.

The list is deliberately uncurated for tractability: a Forward Deployed Engineer role at Anthropic and a vegan-SaaS founding-AE seat are not equally likely, but both are in-bounds given current evidence.

## Implications

- The first three categories (frontier labs, founding AE, GTM eng) are mutually reinforcing — each compounds the others on a CV, and any one of them is a credible next step. Picking among them is mostly a function of risk tolerance and ownership preference, not fit. ^[inferred]
- Categories 4–6 (PM, DevRel, investor) represent **pivots** rather than continuations. Each trades one half of the profile (engineering or selling) for breadth. Worth flagging because they can be entered now but become harder to re-enter the operator track from later. ^[inferred]
- Category 7 (founding) is always on the table given the TU Berlin Center of Entrepreneurship involvement and the productizable existing systems. It is also the only category where Stackgini equity becomes a direct opportunity cost.
- Categories 8 and 9 are useful as **fallback or wildcard** anchors — relevant when the top categories are blocked by visa/comp/geography constraints not yet captured.

## Gaps

Filters not in the wiki that would compress this list significantly:

- **Compensation floor and equity vs. cash preference** — frontier labs and incumbents pay very differently from founding-AE seats.
- **Geography** — Berlin-only, DACH, EU-remote, willing-to-relocate-to-SF? Frontier-lab roles often require relocation.
- **Visa / work-permit constraints** — relevant for US-headquartered roles.
- **Dealbreakers** — anti-lowcode is captured ([[concepts/founding-gtm-engineer-thesis]]) but not other dealbreakers (big-company politics, pre-seed risk tolerance, pure-engineering, pure-sales).
- **Leadership ambition** — IC-forever vs. SDR/AE management vs. founder track. The wiki notes he has hired and ramped 3 SDRs but does not state preference.
- **Vertical preferences beyond AI** — his background spans legal-tech, equities, retail ops; current AI-heavy framing may be over-fitting to recent activity.

Filling these gaps is a prerequisite for turning this map into a shortlist.

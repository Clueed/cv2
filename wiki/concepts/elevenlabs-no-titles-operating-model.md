# ElevenLabs No-Titles Operating Model

> The public operating principle most quoted by ElevenLabs staff and the one that **most directly shapes the [[references/elevenlabs-growth-generalist-jd|Growth Generalist]] role's expectations**. Authored publicly by [[entities/luke-harries|Luke Harries]] (LinkedIn essay, late 2024) and CEO [[entities/mati-staniszewski|Mati Staniszewski]] (officechai interview confirming "it's working well" ~a year in).

## The principles

From the official careers framing and JD body:

> **High-velocity:** Rapid experimentation, lean autonomous teams, minimal bureaucracy.
>
> **Impact not job titles:** We don't have job titles. Instead, it's about the impact you have. No task is above or beneath you.
>
> **AI first:** We use AI to move faster with higher-quality results. We do this across the whole company — from engineering to growth to operations.
>
> **Excellence everywhere:** Everything we do should match the quality of our AI models.
>
> **Global team:** We prioritize your talent, not your location.

## Org shape

- **~20 micro-teams of 5-10 people** (per Mati's officechai interview).
- No middle-manager layer. Each team owns end-to-end output for its surface area.
- **"IOI medalists and ex-founders"** is named in the JD body as part of the team profile — this is the talent bar: competitive-programming AND founder-shape, not corporate-marketer-shape.
- The Head of Growth umbrella ([[entities/luke-harries]]) covers marketing + product + engineering + DevX, which means **growth/marketing is not a siloed function** — it's adjacent to product and developer experience at the team-shape level.

## What "no titles" actually means in practice

Cross-referenced across [[entities/luke-harries|Luke Harries]]'s essay + [[entities/mati-staniszewski|Mati's]] confirmation + multiple Ashby JDs:

| Public surface | Practical reality |
|---|---|
| "We don't have job titles" | Internal directory does not assign titles; LinkedIn titles are individual choice (Luke chose "Head of Growth"; Carles chose "VP Revenue"). |
| "Impact not titles" | Comp + scope are calibrated to **output**, not to band/level frameworks. |
| "No task above or beneath you" | A senior person will write copy, run a billboard negotiation, ship a SQL dashboard — the Growth Generalist JD is **literally a statement of this principle as a role**. |
| Hiring through Ashby with descriptive labels (Growth Generalist, B2B Marketing - UK/I, etc.) | The labels are role-shapes for external candidate discovery, not internal hierarchies. |

## What this means for an applicant

- **Don't frame your story around past job titles.** Frame around shipped impact + ownership scope + outputs. Mark's [[skills/ai-powered-sales-tooling|three flagship sales tools]] are exactly this shape.
- **The seat is the operating principle in role form.** "Surge on the highest-priority projects and help fill the gaps as needed" is the no-titles principle applied to one human. Applicants who want a clear scope/lane will mis-fit.
- **AI-first is a measurable bar.** Luke publicly cites $100K+ saved with AI tooling. Applicants who *use* AI fluently in their work (not just talk about it) signal correctly.
- **"Excellence matches the AI models"** sets the bar at the top of each individual surface — copy, SQL, dashboards, modelling all need to be at high-bar, not "good enough for marketing."
- **Global team / location prioritisation** is the public stance that backs the JD's "remote and can be executed globally" line. Vienna is not on the explicit hub list, but the operating principle says explicitly that location is secondary.

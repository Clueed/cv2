# Voice AI Enterprise TAM — the customer-service wedge

> The market-sizing frame [[entities/elevenlabs|ElevenLabs]] uses publicly (in Mati's [Summit Feb 2026 keynote](https://summit.elevenlabs.io/london) and across enterprise sales material) to position [[entities/elevenlabs|ElevenAgents]] as not-a-narrow-product-but-a-platform-for-a-massive-cost-line.

## The frame

> "Companies worldwide spend over **$400 billion on customer service** every year. The average UK resident spends **10 hours on hold** each year."
>
> — Mati Staniszewski, ElevenLabs Summit London, Feb 2026

This is **one of the largest immediate commercial opportunities in applied AI**. Voice agents that handle Tier-1 support, callbacks, outbound dunning, in-network call assistants, and multilingual routing all attack this $400B line.

## Why it matters for B2B GTM at ElevenLabs

The TAM frame **shapes the entire B2B marketing playbook**:

- **Pipeline math is concentrated, not diffuse.** Each enterprise deployment is large (Revolut's 4M customers, Klarna's 35M US customers, Deutsche Telekom's full subscriber base, IBM's watsonx user base). The economics reward landing a small number of right-sized accounts, not spraying SMB demand-gen.
- **Marketing creative pulls on hold-time, time-to-resolution, and language coverage** — the public hook for ElevenAgents (Revolut: 8x faster, 30+ languages; Klarna: 10x faster) is *always* a metric pulled from this TAM frame.
- **The buyer is contact-centre + customer-experience leadership**, often inside IT/operations functions. Different buyer profile from creative-tools (where the buyer is content/marketing/dev).
- **Influencer + OOH + airport billboards** make sense at this TAM — enterprise CXO + IT-procurement audiences fly through SFO, watch business television, attend RSA / Money 20/20 / MWC. The Growth Generalist JD's mention of "negotiating billboard inventory in SFO airport" reads as **direct ElevenAgents enterprise-buyer ad inventory**, not consumer / creator marketing.

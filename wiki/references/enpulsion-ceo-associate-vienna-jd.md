## What this role actually is

Weighted reading of the JD:

| Pillar | Weight | What it actually is |
|---|---|---|
| Decision-to-execution translation | ~30% | Take CEO discussions and decisions, convert to plans with owners + timelines, track follow-through. Classic PMO / chief-of-staff mechanic. |
| Cross-functional coordination | ~25% | Connector role across functions and international sites; resolve friction; manage dependencies. Influence-without-authority is the load-bearing skill. |
| Executive materials | ~20% | "Advanced PowerPoint" + "create clear, structured presentations and decision materials." Surprisingly prominent in the profile, named twice. |
| Lightweight process design | ~15% | Build systems and processes that improve execution and comms. This is the productization-of-bespoke-work pillar ([[user_productization_affinity]]). |
| CEO proxy / ad-hoc | ~10% | Stand in for the CEO on high-impact projects when needed. Scope and authority not specified. |

Effective role: **execution-partner CoS with a heavy deck-craft component**, sitting in the CEO orbit, no direct reports, no commercial pillar (sales/marketing/customer work is absent from the JD). The "international sites" phrasing suggests ENPULSION has operations beyond Vienna (likely sales/applications support presence in the US — ENPULSION Inc. operates out of Florida — though this is inferred ^[inferred], not stated in the JD).

The CoS connector role is structurally the same skill, exercised internally.
- **Productization affinity** ([[user_productization_affinity]]) — "Build lightweight processes and systems" is explicit JD copy. Mark is drawn to turning bespoke/relational work into systems; the CoS pillar that builds operating infrastructure maps onto that.
- **AI-native ops as differentiator** — three shipped AI sales tools at Stackgini ([[skills/ai-powered-sales-tooling]]) are tangible evidence that "lightweight processes and systems" can be built end-to-end. Most CoS applicants from consulting backgrounds bring deck + Notion, not shipped tools.

- **BSc filter cleared** — CBS Cologne B.A. with Financial Management specialization ([[references/mark-education]]).
- **Vienna hub** — same geography as [[references/allocator-one-investment-manager-vienna-jd|Allocator One]] and [[references/vendure-product-marketing-gtm-vienna-jd|Vendure PMM]]; no relocation overhead.
- **Experience-shape match** — JD names "consulting, high-growth startups, scale-ups, tech companies." Stackgini (B2B SaaS, VC-backed, ~2.5 years founding-GTM) sits inside that frame.

**Stretch:**
- **PowerPoint requirement is explicit and prominent** — named in the profile as "advanced PowerPoint and presentation skills." Mark works almost entirely in Typst on the CV / cover-letter side, and at Stackgini decks are produced via Slides / Pitch / Figma, not PowerPoint specifically. The deeper question is whether the JD is signaling **deck-jockey shaped role** (most days = polishing slides for the CEO) versus deck-as-output of decision support (most days = framing problems, slides are the deliverable). The fact that it appears twice in a short JD (Key Responsibilities + Profile) tips toward the former.
- **Space / propulsion domain has zero reps** — Mark's narrative is B2B SaaS / agentic IT decision-making, not aerospace, propulsion, or hardware. Same kind of domain gap as the FMV deep-tech application but more pointed because ENPULSION sells one product category to a known buyer set; "why ENPULSION, why space, why now" needs a real answer that is not generic curiosity.
- **No commercial pillar in the role** — sales, marketing, customer-facing work are entirely absent from the JD. This is the **off-commercial-est** seat tracked in the wiki: less commercial than [[references/first-momentum-visiting-associate-2027-jd|FMV Visiting Associate]] (which has ~15% portfolio-GTM support), less commercial than [[references/allocator-one-investment-manager-vienna-jd|Allocator One IR]] (which is fundamentally IR comms = external-facing).
- **Comp ambiguous** — €3,415.01 × 14 = ~€47,810 floor; "significant overpayment" likely lands in the €55–75K range for ~2.5 years experience, below the Allocator One €80K floor and well below Vienna AE OTE ceilings. Realistic but not high.
- **"International sites" coordination assumes time-zone tolerance and travel** — neither quantified in the JD. If the secondary site is US (ENPULSION Inc. Florida ^[inferred]), expect either travel or evening calls.

**Calibration** (per [[synthesis/mark-progression-over-direction]]): roughly ~15-20% commercial / ~80-85% off-commercial. The role is operational and CEO-orbit, not customer-facing. **This is a bigger directional swing off the engineer-seller commercial baseline than FMV** (~30% commercial), Allocator One (~70%), Vendure PMM (~80%), or ElevenLabs Growth Generalist (~90%).

The progression question (per the synthesis): is the **seat at month 13** in a CoS role at a Vienna-area space-tech scaleup more valuable than the month-13 seat from a permanent commercial role (next-tier AE, founding PMM, Growth Generalist)? CoS roles famously bifurcate: **good CoS** → "general manager in training" (next steps: BU lead, COO, founder); **mid CoS** → "deck monkey" stuck inside the CEO's calendar with no P&L visibility. The PowerPoint emphasis in this JD is a yellow flag for the second shape; the "act as proxy when needed" + "build systems" framing is the green flag for the first. Cannot resolve without screen-level diligence.

## Application angle if pursued

Differentiator vs typical CoS applicants (consulting / strategy / scale-up generalist):

1. **Founding GTM > consultant** — Mark has lived inside a founding team for ~2.5 years and contributed to commercial, customer, and fundraising work simultaneously. CoS applicants from consulting bring frameworks; Mark brings reps from a real operating environment, including reps inside CEO/board meetings and investor calls. This is "honest, structured input to improve decision quality" already exercised.
2. **Productization is real** — the systems-and-processes pillar of the JD is exactly where Mark's three AI sales tools ([[skills/ai-powered-sales-tooling]]) sit. Lead with one concrete tool (the ABM data layer or account research engine), tie it to "lightweight processes and systems that improve execution and communication." Most CoS candidates do not arrive having shipped TypeScript/Python on raw LLM APIs.
3. **Cross-functional orchestration via intra-IT buying centers** ([[feedback_stackgini_buying_centers]]) — translate this into the language of internal coordination: "I've spent two years getting EA, InfoSec, ITAM, and FinOps to align on a single decision, externally, without authority. The same mechanic applies internally to engineering, ops, and applications-support."
4. **Founder-side capital-formation reps** ([[entities/mark-vavulov]] fundraising section) give Mark cap-table / DDQ / commercial-DD / term-sheet exposure — atypical for a CoS candidate and useful at a scaleup likely working toward later-stage rounds or strategic M&A in the propulsion segment.
5. **Why-ENPULSION narrative gap** — needs a real answer. Generic "I'm interested in deep tech" will not differentiate. Plausible angles to develop pre-application: (a) the modular FEEP architecture as a productization story (bespoke aerospace → modular catalog product is the same shape Mark is drawn to); (b) the small-sat market's commercial inflection (constellation operators are buying propulsion in volume; growth-stage scaling problems are tractable for a CoS); (c) honest "I want a CoS shape, here's why your stage and structure fit" rather than space-tech love.

**Counter-signals to weigh before applying:**
- The deck-craft prominence in the JD is a real flag. If the screen confirms the role is ~40%+ slide work, this is the deck-monkey shape, not the GM-in-training shape, and the month-13 progression argument weakens substantially.
- No commercial pillar means Mark's compounding engineer-seller leverage stops compounding inside this seat. The progression argument is purely "CoS-as-launchpad," which requires the role to be the green-flag shape, not the yellow-flag shape.
- Comp probably lands €55–75K — below the Allocator One floor and well below Vienna AE OTE ceilings. Acceptable for the right seat shape; bad for the wrong one.

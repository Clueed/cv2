> **About First Momentum Ventures**

>
> As a Visiting Associate, you'll be a full member of our investment team with real ownership from day one and direct exposure to how the venture capital world truly operates.

It's the perfect launchpad for ambitious talent aiming to become a Chief of Staff at a leading European startup, land a full-time VC role, or start their own company down the road.

> - **Lead deep-dive research**: Explore emerging technologies and market trends every week. Conduct sharp, data-driven analysis and present insights that influence our investment theses and strategic direction.

> - You're genuinely curious about deeply technical domains. Whether you have an engineering background or just love learning how breakthrough technologies work, you're energized by technical depth.

## What this role actually is

Weighted reading of the JD:

| Pillar | Weight | What it actually is |
|---|---|---|
| Sourcing & first calls | ~35% | Independent founder outreach, first-call conviction, feeding the top of the funnel. The scout network plus the Visiting Associate is the deal-flow engine. |
| Deal-flow execution | ~30% | Sitting in the full process (first contact → DD → IC → close) as a junior member of the investment team. Evaluation contribution, not decision-making. |
| Thesis research | ~20% | Weekly deep-dives on emerging tech/markets. Output: written analysis influencing investment theses. |
| Portfolio support | ~15% | Hands-on GTM, AI workflow automation, ops projects with portfolio founders. This is the part Mark's existing toolkit ([[skills/ai-powered-sales-tooling]]) maps onto directly. |

Effective role: **junior associate with sourcing + research as primary outputs**, framed as a launchpad rather than a tenure-track investor seat. Fixed-term by design.

## Fit notes for Mark

**Strong:**

- **German + English fluent** — required gate cleared.
- **Engineer-seller story** maps onto two pillars directly: (1) "move from technical papers to business models to founder conversations" matches Mark's Stackgini buying-center work ([[entities/stackgini]]) where intra-IT technical buyers are the room; (2) "automating workflows with AI" for portfolio companies is exactly what Mark does at Stackgini ([[skills/ai-powered-sales-tooling]]) — likely a credible differentiator vs other Visiting Associate applicants.
- **Productization affinity** ([[user_productization_affinity]]) — portfolio support pillar explicitly mentions "automating workflows with AI and optimizing operations." This is the bespoke-to-systems shape Mark is drawn to.
- **Fundraising-support track record** ([[entities/mark-vavulov]] fundraising section) gives Mark cap-table / DDQ / term-sheet exposure from the founder side — atypical for Visiting Associate applicants from consulting or generalist startup backgrounds.

**Stretch:**
- **It's a fixed-term role, not permanent.** 6–12 months from Jan 2027. The framing is explicit: it's a launchpad into full-time VC / CoS / founder, not the seat itself. This is a different shape from the Allocator One IR seat or a founding GTM role.
- **Comp ambiguous** — "well above an internship salary" with no number. Almost certainly below the Allocator One €80K floor, and well below Vienna AE OTE. The bet is on what the seat enables in months 13+, not on year-one cash.
- **No prior VC reps** — the JD signals openness to consulting / startup / technical backgrounds, but candidates from VC-adjacent paths (CoS at a portfolio company, banking analyst, MBA-track) will compete on signal Mark doesn't carry.
- **Genuine interest in deep tech** — Mark's current narrative is AI-GTM / engineer-seller, not climate/manufacturing/quantum/aerospace. He'd need a *why-now-why-this-fund* narrative beyond "venture-curious."

**Calibration** (per [[synthesis/mark-progression-over-direction]]): roughly ~30% commercial / ~70% off-commercial — sourcing and research dominate, with the portfolio-support pillar as the only direct commercial-skill carryover. This is a meaningful shift away from the commercial-adjacent next-step bias the filter usually points at. The progression question is whether the **seat at month 13** (full-time VC role at FMV or a peer fund, CoS at an FMV portfolio company, founding role coming out of the deep-tech network) is more valuable than the month-13 seat from a permanent commercial role (next-tier AE, CSM lead, founding PMM). VC-launchpad seats have famously asymmetric payoff distributions; the median outcome is "back to operating" with a network upgrade.

- vs **[[references/allocator-one-investment-manager-vienna-jd|Allocator One IR Vienna]]** — Allocator One is a permanent IR-comms / fund-modeling seat; FMV is a fixed-term sourcing-and-research rotation. Allocator One is closer to Mark's commercial baseline; FMV is the bigger directional swing.

## Application angle if pursued

The differentiator vs typical Visiting Associate applicants (consulting + master's + internships):
1. **Operator track, not analyst track** — Mark has run a founding GTM role for ~2.5 years at a B2B SaaS, including raise support across pre-seed and seed. Most Visiting Associate candidates have built decks; Mark has been in the room for both sides of an LP/investor cycle from the founder's side.
2. **AI-native portfolio support is real** — point to [[skills/ai-powered-sales-tooling]] as evidence that "automating workflows with AI" for portfolio companies is something Mark can do on day one, not learn.
3. **Intra-IT buying-center work** ([[entities/stackgini]]) is a credible analogue for evaluating B2B deep-tech founders' commercial readiness — "can this team sell into the buyer they claim?" is a question Mark has been on the *implementing* side of.
4. **Why-now narrative gap** — needs a genuine "why deep tech, why now, why FMV" frame. Generic VC-curiosity will not differentiate. Possible angles: (a) the productization filter ([[user_productization_affinity]]) — pre-seed deep tech is where bespoke research becomes products; (b) the engineer-seller frame ([[concepts/founding-gtm-engineer-thesis]]) reads the same buyer dynamic FMV portfolio companies will face.

## Tailoring decisions visible in this snapshot

What this Allocator One package does differently than the [[references/anthropic-final-resume-2026-05|Anthropic snapshot]]:

**On the CV side:**

- **Stackgini bullets collapsed from six to three.** The Anthropic CV used six SDR/playbook/AE-process-flavored bullets to load JD keywords. For Allocator One those signals don't read; instead the three remaining bullets are (1) scale + multi-stakeholder closing, (2) fundraising-support track record, (3) AI agent workflows. The middle bullet is new for this round and is the load-bearing piece against the "investment manager" framing.
- **Bullet 2 is the fundraising bullet.** Lifted from the facts surfaced in the 2026-05-31 capture session (see [[entities/mark-vavulov]] "Fundraising support" section). Explicit list of artifacts owned — VC sourcing, first calls, decks, data room governance, DD Q&A, customer reference calls, term-sheet and cap-table review — matches the JD's "owns end to end, from the first investor touchpoint to the signed commitment" framing onto the founder-side seat Mark actually has. Does **not** claim waterfalls, LP-side experience, or building a fund model from scratch (per [[feedback_finance_claims_scope]]).
- **AI bullet leans into "background of daily operations, not one-off experiments"** — verbatim phrasing match with the JD's "running in the background of the fundraising operation, not as one-off experiments". Word-for-word lift.
- **Luther collapsed to the Finance role only.** Luther's IT track is dropped entirely; only the Financial Operations & Controlling Intern entry remains, with one HGB-focused bullet. This is the merge guidance from the 2026-05-31 capture: for finance- and IR-shaped JDs, the Finance entry is the only Luther entry that should appear.
- **"VC-backed" added to Stackgini description.** Signals fundraising relevance up-front.
- **Tendenz description simplified.** "z-score-based statistical significance of daily returns" is the same idea as the Anthropic version but compressed; minor.
- **Interests list adds "Cryptocurrency, on-chain finance"** at the top — signal toward the JD's "tokenization or digital assets in a private markets context" nice-to-have.
- **TU Berlin and JCU bullets cleaned up.** Less technical-coursework signaling; JCU bullet now explicitly names the pivot trigger.

**On the cover-letter side:**

- **Productization is the opener.** Per [[user_productization_affinity]], turning bespoke/relational work into systems and products is a positive role-fit signal for Mark. The cover letter's hook ("you are productizing what is normally a bespoke, per-fund relationship") is genuine, not retrofitted.
- **Fund-econ gate addressed obliquely**, not head-on. Mark does **not** claim he can "build a VC/PE model from scratch and explain it clearly in conversation" — that's the JD's hard gate and he is not yet credible on it. Instead the letter frames it as "The fund-side seat is new; the deal mechanics are not" — claiming founder-side deal mechanics, degree, and Excel/Ops while declining the fund-side claim. This matches the [[feedback_finance_claims_scope]] discipline.
- **"20x'd ARR with two sales FTEs"** is a sharper restatement of the AI-leverage claim than any single CV bullet carries. Fits cover-letter density.
- **Closing is direct, no hedging.** "I recommend meeting with me if only to hear my thoughts on AI automation from first principles. You won't regret it." — aligns with [[feedback_no_self_hedging]]: state credentials as direct claims.
- **No em dashes** anywhere in the letter (or the DM). [[feedback_no_em_dashes]] held.

**On the LinkedIn DM side:**

- **German, not English.** Matches the recipient's regional context. English would have read as overly formal for a LinkedIn DM after a connect.
- **Mirrors the JD ordering as the bridge.** "Ihr nennt AI Automations als ersten Punkt" treats the JD's own structure as the evidence that the fit reads.
- **No restated CV.** The DM does not duplicate the cover letter; it does the connect-acknowledge → app-confirm → fit-signal → meeting-ask sequence.

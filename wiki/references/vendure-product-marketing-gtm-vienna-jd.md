## Job Description (verbatim, pasted 2026-06-01)

> **Product Marketing & GTM Manager (f/m/x)**
> Vienna (Hybrid)
> Full-time, Permanent employee
> 55,000 - 70,000 € per year
>
> **Intro**
>
> Vendure is an enterprise commerce platform without the trade-offs. One coherent, extensible backend that lets technical teams move at speed and stay in full control, instead of choosing between rigid legacy systems and complex DIY stacks.
>
> We started as an open-source commerce framework with an active developer community, and we are now expanding into a commercial enterprise platform with Vendure Platform and Vendure Cloud.
>
> Our strongest fit is with companies whose commerce requirements do not fit a standard template: complex B2B, marketplaces, custom pricing, ERP-heavy workflows, multi-brand setups, and other business models that need to model commerce the way they actually operate, without rebuilding everything from scratch.
>
> We are looking for our first dedicated Product Marketing & GTM hire to help turn this transition into clear messaging, better lead conversion, stronger sales materials, and qualified pipeline.
>
> **What we're looking for**
>
> *Product positioning and messaging*
> You will help us clearly communicate the difference between:
> - Vendure Core: our open-source commerce framework
> - Vendure Platform: our enterprise commerce platform
> - Vendure Cloud: our managed Vendure offering
> - Vendure Core Production Support: our support offer for serious Core users
>
> Your work will help buyers understand which Vendure product is right for them without diluting our enterprise platform positioning.
>
> *Demand capture*
> You will improve how we convert existing demand into the right commercial path. This includes:
> - improving website messaging and conversion,
> - refining the contact form and lead qualification flow,
> - creating landing pages for high-intent use cases,
> - helping route leads into the right offer,
> - supporting email follow-up and nurture flows,
> - working on comparison and migration pages,
> - improving how we turn inbound interest into qualified opportunities.
>
> *Sales enablement*
> You will create practical material that helps us sell Vendure Platform, Vendure Cloud, and support offerings more clearly. Examples:
> - sales decks,
> - one-pagers,
> - case-study slides,
> - objection-handling material,
> - competitor comparison notes,
> - Platform Pilot material,
> - Core Production Support proposal templates,
> - pricing and packaging explanations.
>
> *Content and customer proof*
> You will turn Vendure's product strengths and customer stories into useful market-facing content. This may include:
> - case studies,
> - blog posts,
> - customer stories,
> - product explainers,
> - solution pages,
> - webinar content,
> - LinkedIn posts,
> - founder-led content support,
> - partner co-marketing material.
>
> *Partner and agency enablement*
> Agencies and implementation partners are an important part of Vendure's go-to-market motion. You will help create the material partners need to position Vendure with their clients. This may include:
> - partner pitch decks,
> - "Why Vendure" materials,
> - co-marketing assets,
> - implementation use-case pages,
> - partner onboarding content,
> - webinar and event support.
>
> *Marketing operations and reporting*
> You will help make our GTM work measurable. This includes working with tools such as HubSpot or similar systems to track:
> - lead source,
> - lead quality,
> - product fit,
> - conversion by segment,
> - pipeline influence,
> - campaign performance,
> - website conversion,
> - content and landing-page performance.
>
> You do not need to be a full RevOps expert, but you should be comfortable working with CRM, forms, dashboards, and basic funnel reporting.
>
> **Why us?**
> - A meaningful role in shaping Vendure's transition from open-source commerce framework to enterprise commerce platform.
> - Direct collaboration with the founders and product team.
> - High ownership and visibility.
> - A technical product with an active open-source community.
> - Real customer and partner traction.
> - Flexible working setup.
> - Salary range of €55,000–€70,000 gross/year, depending on experience.
> - Potential performance-based bonus.
> - Opportunity to grow with the company as the GTM function develops.
>
> **About us**
>
> Vendure is an enterprise commerce platform without the trade-offs. One coherent, extensible backend that lets technical teams move at speed and stay in full control, instead of choosing between rigid legacy systems and complex DIY stacks.
>
> We started as an open-source commerce framework with an active developer community, and we are now expanding into a commercial enterprise platform with Vendure Platform and Vendure Cloud.
>
> Our strongest fit is with companies whose commerce requirements do not fit a standard template: complex B2B, marketplaces, custom pricing, ERP-heavy workflows, multi-brand setups, and other business models that need to model commerce the way they actually operate, without rebuilding everything from scratch.

## New signals from the verbatim text (vs. earlier WebFetch summary)

The verbatim JD added several specifics that the summary elided. These are load-bearing for the cover letter:

1. **"first dedicated Product Marketing & GTM hire"** — explicit confirmation this is a founding seat. Removes any ambiguity about role shape.
2. **"complex B2B, marketplaces, custom pricing, ERP-heavy workflows, multi-brand setups"** — *multi-brand setups* is new and absent from public marketing copy on vendure.io. Worth probing in the interview: what's the multi-brand customer pattern?
3. **"Platform Pilot material"** — there's a named **Platform Pilot** motion. Almost certainly the time-boxed evaluation step before a full Platform commit. Worth asking about; the marketing assets for this are specific (pilot scopes, success criteria, conversion-to-Platform criteria).
4. **"Core Production Support proposal templates"** — proposal templates for the support tier are an explicit deliverable. Implies real volume of Core users converting to paid support.
5. **"Direct collaboration with the founders and product team"** — reporting line implicit: David Höck (commercial) + Michael Bromley (product narrative). Not stated to a head of marketing (because there isn't one — this hire effectively *is* the marketing function).
6. **"You do not need to be a full RevOps expert"** — explicit softening on the ops side; ops fluency is expected but RevOps depth is not the bar.
7. **"comparison and migration pages"** — explicit. Aligns with the competitive comparison work (vs. Shopify, Commercetools, Medusa, WooCommerce, Magento) flagged in the deep-dive.
8. **NO formal requirements list.** No years-of-experience gate, no must-have credentials, no language proficiency requirement. The JD lists *responsibilities* and *upside*, then stops. Unusual signal: the founders are hiring for fit and attitude, not for a credential filter. **This is a meaningful unlock for Mark's profile.**
9. **"Potential performance-based bonus"** + **"Opportunity to grow with the company"** — comp upside language but no equity mentioned. Surface equity question in the screen.

## Role-shape read

This is a **generalist PMM / GTM-of-one seat**, not an AE / sales seat and not a senior PMM. Weighted reading:

| Pillar | Weight | What it actually is |
|---|---|---|
| Demand capture + marketing ops | ~30% | Website conversion, forms, lead routing, HubSpot dashboards. Founder-stage demand-gen ops. |
| Sales enablement | ~25% | Building the material library that the (presumably small) sales team consumes. Decks, objection handlers, pricing explainers. |
| Product positioning | ~20% | Owning the four-SKU story; the load-bearing strategic piece, but bounded — the positioning question is already mostly answered, this is about systematising it. |
| Content & customer proof | ~15% | Case studies, blog, founder content. Production work. |
| Partner enablement | ~10% | Agency/implementer co-marketing — episodic, event-driven. |

Net: a **founding GTM-marketing seat** that compounds into PMM Lead / Head of Marketing if Vendure scales — but the floor is *production marketer with CRM duty*, not strategist. Closer in shape to a Founding GTM hire at an OSS-to-enterprise startup than to either a pure PMM or an AE.

- **Founding-GTM ownership across pillars** — Mark's Stackgini work spans content, demand-gen tooling, ABM data layer, founder-led outbound, and sales enablement materials. The six pillars of this role overlap heavily with what he already does, just relabelled "marketing."
- **Technical-audience writing** — Stackgini sells to intra-IT buying centres ([[entities/stackgini]] [[feedback_stackgini_buying_centers]]); writing for technical buyers is a daily skill. Vendure's audience is similar shape (engineering / commerce-platform leads).

**Stretch:**
- **Role shape is PMM, not AE.** Mark's track is founding-GTM-with-AE-motion. PMM emphasises positioning research, message testing, win/loss analysis, asset production, less direct deal ownership. The skill overlap is high but the seat-shape switch is real — interview will probe whether he wants to step back from the deal table.
- **Comp floor: €55–70K.** Lower than Allocator One's €80K + bonus and well below Vienna AE OTE (~$270K ceiling per [[synthesis/mark-job-direction-map]]). The bet would be on (a) progression to Head of Marketing as Vendure scales, (b) the OSS-to-commercial inflection compounding, (c) Vienna geography hard-constraint making this preferable to remote-only options.

- **No equity disclosed.** OSS-to-enterprise pivots typically have meaningful equity for early commercial hires, but the public listing doesn't mention it. Need to surface in screen.

**Calibration**: Higher landing odds than Allocator One — the skill overlap is more direct (HubSpot, demand-gen, content, founder-adjacent) and there's no fund-econ gate to clear. Probably **25–40% to interview** if applied cleanly. ^[inferred]

# Remote (remote.com) — GTM Strategy (Strategic Business Partner)

**Title posted**: GTM Strategy (team: GTM Strategy; no specific level in the title — JD notes "job titles may span more than one career level")
**Team**: GTM Strategy
**Reports to**: Head of GTM Strategy
**Location**: 100% Remote; applications prioritized from EMEA and Americas
**Type**: Full-time
**Comp**: $52,600 – $147,950 USD (geo-ranged global band; actual offer depends on location/level)
**Source**: Remote careers (Greenhouse), captured 2026-06-21 from user paste
**JD URL**: [greenhouse.io/remotecom/jobs/7645176003](https://job-boards.greenhouse.io/remotecom/jobs/7645176003)

This page captures the JD verbatim and analyses the seat against [[synthesis/mark-progression-over-direction]] and [[synthesis/mark-job-direction-map]]. It is the **first "strategy / BizOps" JD** tracked in the wiki — a strategic-business-partner seat gated on a consulting / strategy-ops background rather than on closing reps or systems-building. It is the closest sibling to [[references/anthropic-gtm-strategy-ops-dach-jd|Anthropic's GTM Strategy & Ops role]], but with a materially more reachable experience bar.

## Job Description (verbatim)

>
> Remote is solving modern organizations' biggest challenge – navigating global employment compliantly with ease. We make it possible for businesses of all sizes to recruit, pay, and manage international teams. [...] all of our positions are fully remote.

Depending on business needs and candidate strengths, this role may support leaders across Marketing, Sales, or Partnerships.

> - Ability to generate insights from data and connect them to actionable strategic recommendations
> - Strong leadership and ownership skills, and the ability to work autonomously while linking back to overall business strategy
> - Experience leading complex initiatives end-to-end, from hypothesis generation to solution implementation
> - Excellent written and verbal communication skills in English, including the ability to influence senior stakeholders
> - Intellectual curiosity, critical thinking, and a bias for action
> - Collaborative mindset and adaptability in a fast-changing environment
> - Comfort with ambiguity and a willingness to dive deep into unstructured business problems
> - Passion for technology and understanding of trends and market dynamics in SaaS and B2B software markets
> - Great senior stakeholder management skills
> - Demonstrates strong automation and AI capabilities and AI fluency.

> - **Explicit Strategic Business Partnering**: Serve as a dedicated strategic business partner to one or more GTM leaders, helping identify opportunities, solve business challenges, and drive execution against strategic priorities.
> - **Commercialization**: Partner with Product, PMM, Pricing, and Enablement teams to assess commercial readiness and support the successful commercialization of new products, capabilities, and strategic initiatives.

> - Influence senior stakeholders to gain buy-in for strategic recommendations and drive execution across the organisation
> - Lead cross-functional strategic initiatives end-to-end, from problem definition to alignment, execution, and performance measurement
> - Build trusted relationships with cross-functional leaders and executives to proactively surface and shape high-priority initiatives
> - Act as a generalist thought partner to revenue leaders, helping them solve complex business challenges

## What this role actually is

Weighted reading of the JD:

| Pillar | Weight | What it actually is |
|---|---|---|
| Strategic business partnering | ~35% | Dedicated thought partner to one or more GTM leaders (Marketing / Sales / Partnerships). Surface opportunities, frame problems, drive execution against their priorities. This is the load-bearing pillar; the seat is defined by who you partner with, not a fixed remit. |
| Structured analysis → recommendation | ~25% | Quantitative + qualitative analysis of revenue performance and customer behavior to find growth/efficiency opportunities; assess trade-offs; synthesize into C-level-ready strategy. This is the consulting-toolkit core (frame, analyze, recommend). |
| Commercialization support | ~20% | Partner with Product / PMM / Pricing / Enablement to assess commercial readiness and launch new products/capabilities. GTM-to-product bridge. |
| Cross-functional initiative leadership | ~15% | Lead initiatives end-to-end: problem definition → alignment → execution → performance measurement. Owns outcomes across functions without owning the functions. |
| Executive influence / relationship-building | ~5% (pervasive) | Influence senior stakeholders to gain buy-in; build trusted exec relationships to proactively shape the initiative pipeline. Not a discrete workstream but a constant requirement. |

Effective role: an **internal strategy consultant / BizOps operator embedded in the GTM org**, mapped onto one or more revenue leaders as their analytical-and-execution force multiplier. It is the in-house-McKinsey-for-GTM shape: no direct revenue quota, no managerial headcount named, no P&L ^[inferred] — leverage comes through influence and shipped recommendations, not authority.

The "may support leaders across Marketing, Sales, or Partnerships" line means the **specific remit is assigned post-hire** based on business need and candidate strength. The seat is deliberately generalist; the interview loop (which ends with "either VP, Growth Marketing or General Manager, Partnership Business") signals the two most likely landing zones are growth-marketing or partnerships.

The interview loop is **consulting-style**: recruiter → hiring manager → **take-home case study** → **case presentation to SVP Revenue/Strategy/RevOps + Head of GTM Strategy** → functional-leader interview → bar raiser → employment verification. The take-home + live case defense is the load-bearing screen, exactly as an MBB or strategy-house final round would be.

## Fit notes for Mark

**Strong:**
- **The experience gate is reachable, unlike Anthropic's.** The bar is "top-tier strategy consulting *OR* a strategic role within a high-growth startup or scale-up (strategy, business operations, corporate development, growth)." Mark has no consulting pedigree, but ~2.5 years as founding GTM operator at [[entities/stackgini]] — owning strategy, ops, and growth in a high-growth scale-up — is a literal instance of the second branch. This is the **cleanest "scale-up strategic role" qualifier** of any JD tracked, and it sidesteps the 10+-years wall that makes [[references/anthropic-gtm-strategy-ops-dach-jd|Anthropic]] a long-shot.
- **AI fluency is a hard requirement, and it is Mark's edge.** "Demonstrates strong automation and AI capabilities and AI fluency" plus "we have built Automation and AI capabilities into the requirements for every role." Most strategy/BizOps applicants treat AI as a buzzword; Mark has shipped three production AI systems on raw LLM APIs ([[skills/ai-powered-sales-tooling]]). This is a differentiator a consulting-background competitor cannot easily match.
- **Fully remote, EMEA prioritized.** Zero relocation friction from Berlin; EMEA is an explicitly prioritized region. Removes the geography constraint that gates US-headquartered frontier-lab roles ([[synthesis/mark-job-direction-map]] Gaps).
- **Commercial-adjacent, compounds existing expertise.** Per [[synthesis/mark-progression-over-direction]], the next-step bias is toward commercial-adjacent seats that compound GTM domain depth. A GTM Strategy seat is exactly that: it generalizes the GTM expertise from "I run one function" to "I advise across the whole revenue org." Strong progression vector toward Strategy/BizOps leadership or VP-track.
- **B2B SaaS domain match.** "Passion for technology and understanding of trends and market dynamics in SaaS and B2B software markets." Stackgini is B2B SaaS; Mark's enterprise reps (DHL, Siemens Healthineers, Danaher, REWE, HAYS) are exactly the market this asks about.
- **End-to-end ownership reps.** "Lead complex initiatives end-to-end, from hypothesis generation to solution implementation" — Mark built the entire GTM function from zero, the strongest possible end-to-end-ownership claim. Building the ABM scoring database and SDR ramp programs are concrete hypothesis-to-implementation artifacts.
- **Data-to-recommendation reps.** The ICP scoring database ([[project_stackgini_gtm_stack]]) is a data-driven segmentation artifact; the funnel/pipeline reporting Mark built feeds the "analyze revenue performance → find opportunities" pillar.

**Stretch:**
- **"Strategist" vs "operator" framing gap.** The role wants someone who *frames and recommends* (consulting muscle), not someone who *builds and sells* (operator muscle). Mark's reps are overwhelmingly hands-on execution; the JD's "structured problem-solving," "assess trade-offs," "synthesize for C-level" language is consulting-deck work. Mark can do this, but his evidence base is operator-shaped, and the **take-home case study is precisely the screen that tests the consulting muscle he has least formal practice in**. This is the single biggest risk axis.
- **No C-level stakeholder-management reps at scale.** The JD repeatedly stresses influencing senior stakeholders and "exposure to C-level decision-making." Mark has worked closely with Stackgini's founders (effectively C-level of a small company), but has not partnered with or presented to the C-suite of a large org. The reps exist in miniature, not at the scale the JD implies. ^[inferred]
- **"Extensive experience" reads senior.** Although the OR-branch is reachable, "extensive experience in a strategic role" plus "may span more than one career level" suggests the band's midpoint targets someone with more than ~2.5 years. Mark would be applying at the junior end of the range; the comp offer would likely sit in the lower half of the $52.6K–$147.95K band for an EMEA hire. ^[inferred]
- **Generalist seat, remit assigned post-hire.** "May support leaders across Marketing, Sales, or Partnerships" means Mark cannot pre-optimize the pitch to a known remit. Upside: flexible. Downside: the seat's progression depends on which leader he is assigned to, which is outside his control at application time. The progression question ([[synthesis/mark-progression-over-direction]]) is therefore partly unresolvable pre-offer.
- **EOR / HR-tech domain has zero reps.** Remote sells global-employment/EOR; Mark sells agentic enterprise IT decision-making ([[feedback_stackgini_buying_centers]]). Both are horizontal B2B SaaS, so the *market-dynamics* understanding transfers, but the specific buyer, ACV, and motion do not. Lower-weight gap than at a vertical company, since this is a strategy seat not a selling seat.
- **HubSpot, not Salesforce / consulting analytics stack.** The JD doesn't name a CRM, but a strategy seat at a scaled EOR company will lean on whatever data stack they run (likely Salesforce + a BI tool). Mark's tooling is HubSpot + custom scripts; the transition to enterprise BI/SQL-heavy analysis is a learnable gap. ^[inferred]

**Calibration** (per [[synthesis/mark-progression-over-direction]]): roughly ~75% commercial / ~25% off-commercial. It sits on the commercial wing (the entire remit is revenue growth), but the *mode* of work is analytical-strategic rather than selling or systems-building. Less commercial-pure than [[references/fonio-gtm-engineer-jd|fonio]] (~95%) or [[references/elevenlabs-growth-generalist-jd|ElevenLabs]] (~90%); more commercial than [[references/enpulsion-ceo-associate-vienna-jd|ENPULSION CoS]] (~15%). It is the **clearest "GTM-strategy / BizOps" instance** tracked — Category 8 of [[synthesis/mark-job-direction-map]] made concrete.

**Interview-odds estimate**: ~20-30% to first screen. The OR-branch qualifier is genuinely met and the AI-fluency requirement is a real differentiator, which lifts this above the Anthropic long-shot. But the field for a remote, EMEA-and-Americas, no-relocation strategy seat at a well-known scaleup is **deep and consulting-heavy** — Mark competes against ex-MBB and ex-scale-up-strategy candidates who have the exact deck-and-case muscle the loop screens for. The take-home case is the great equalizer: if Mark reaches it, the AI-native operator angle plus a genuinely rigorous case can outperform a polished-but-generic consultant; if the screen filters on pedigree first, the operator background is a disadvantage. Net: applyable, but the case study is the whole game.

## Application angle if pursued

Differentiator vs typical applicants (mostly ex-consultants and scale-up strategy analysts with deck polish but thin building/AI reps):

1. **AI-native operator, not AI-curious strategist.** Lead the differentiation on the explicit "automation and AI capabilities" requirement. Most strategy candidates name-drop AI; Mark has shipped three production systems on raw LLM APIs ([[skills/ai-powered-sales-tooling]]) and built an ICP-scoring database ([[project_stackgini_gtm_stack]]). For a company that put AI into *every role's* requirements, "I build the AI, I don't just recommend it" is the sharpest possible wedge.
2. **Founding-GTM = strategy-and-execution in one person.** Reframe the Stackgini story as the scale-up-strategic-role branch of the qualifier: not "I was a seller," but "I owned GTM strategy, segmentation, and execution end-to-end from zero to $1M+ ARR." The $50K → $1M+ ARR claim, the ABM segmentation framework, and the SDR ramp/productivity program are the three hypothesis-to-implementation artifacts that map cleanly onto the JD's pillars.
3. **Quantitative rigor is real, not claimed.** The IFRS R&D-capitalization thesis ([[references/mark-education]]) + tendenz z-score equity tool ([[references/tendenz-project]]) + funnel/pipeline analytics are concrete evidence of the "structured quantitative analysis" the case study will test. Surface these before the case, not after.
4. **The take-home case is the application.** Given that pedigree is the weak axis and the case is the strong-equalizer screen, the entire strategy should be: get to the case, then over-deliver with genuine rigor and an AI-augmented analytical approach that a consultant wouldn't think to show. Do not lead the early screens with "I'm not from consulting" energy; lead with the scale-up-operator-who-ships framing.
5. **Why-Remote narrative**: (a) AI-native company that bakes automation into every role — direct match to how Mark already works; (b) horizontal B2B SaaS at scale lets him generalize GTM expertise from one function to the whole revenue org (the progression case); (c) fully-remote async culture fits a self-directed builder. Avoid generic "I want strategy exposure" — anchor on the specific AI-operator-meets-GTM-strategy intersection.

**Counter-signals to weigh before applying:**
- **Consulting-case muscle is the least-practiced skill in Mark's kit**, and the loop is built around it (take-home + live defense to an SVP). This is the real risk; worth a practice case or two before submitting if pursued seriously.
- **Comp band is enormous and geo-ranged.** $52.6K–$147.95K means the EMEA / junior-end offer could land well below the headline ceiling. Clarify the actual band for an EMEA hire at Mark's level early, or the opportunity-cost-vs-Stackgini math is unknowable.
- **Remit assigned post-hire** (Marketing / Sales / Partnerships). Progression depends on the assignment; partly outside Mark's control. Probe in the screen which leader the seat is most likely to support.
- **"Extensive experience" + multi-level band** means Mark is applying at the junior edge; manage level expectations. The realistic landing is the entry rung of the strategy track, not a senior strategist seat.

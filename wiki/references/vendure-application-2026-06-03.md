Without VC growth pressure, the incentives stay closer to the community by default. I would love to spend the next phase of my career on the GTM side of that problem.

## Tailoring decisions visible in this snapshot

What this Vendure package does differently than the [[references/allocator-one-application-2026-05-31|Allocator One snapshot]] and the [[references/anthropic-final-resume-2026-05|Anthropic snapshot]]:

**On the CV side:**

- **Stackgini bullets expanded back to seven**. Allocator One collapsed to three bullets to spotlight scale + fundraising + AI agents for the IR seat. Vendure's JD spans six PMM pillars (positioning, demand capture, sales enablement, content, partner enablement, marketing ops), so the CV restores the full seven-bullet range so each pillar has a direct line-item match. Bullets 2, 3, 6, and 7 do the heaviest lifting against the JD: HubSpot/AI ops, positioning + funnel reporting, partner channel build, and marketing-surface + sales-narrative authorship.
- **"VC-backed" descriptor dropped**. Vendure is bootstrapped and just torched €3M of agency revenue to stay that way; leading with Stackgini as VC-backed would land oddly. The Stackgini description is the role-shape line ("B2B SaaS for agentic enterprise IT decision-making") with no funding qualifier.
- **Fundraising-support bullet dropped**. Specific to Allocator One; not load-bearing for a PMM seat.
- **Luther entry kept full**. Both the IT Project Management role and the Financial Operations & Controlling internship are present without bullets; the entry is two dates, two titles, no detail. The Allocator One CV collapsed Luther to Finance-only with a single HGB bullet, because the JD demanded financial-modeling proof. For Vendure neither track is load-bearing, so the entry is reduced to a presence-only block with no bullet weight competing for the page.
- **Interests reordered**. Open-source / weight LLM models is first, then "Open source" again as a standalone term, then developer tooling and web development. The list is engineered to land on Vendure's developer-first identity. Crypto / on-chain remains but moves below the OSS and developer-tooling clusters.
- **Tendenz framing kept generic**. Allocator One sharpened the tendenz bullet to emphasise the quantitative-markets read. For Vendure the original full-stack-build framing is retained because the *built and deployed independently, full-stack* signal is the more relevant one for an engineering-aware audience.

**On the cover-letter side:**

- **Three paragraphs, not five**. The strategy doc recommended three; the letter holds the discipline.
- **Para 1 — Why-now hook**: opens with the Stackgini scale claim ($20K → $1M+ at 3x YoY) as the credential, then immediately pivots into the Feb 2026 letter reference and *that is the seat I want*. Front-loads the strongest single number Mark has while keeping the Feb-letter reference load-bearing.
- **Para 2 — Design end of GTM**: replaces an available "concrete proof on AI sales tooling" beat with a higher-altitude statement of *where Mark wants to spend his time*. The four examples (positioning that self-routes, comparison pages, compounding sales material, internal tooling) map 1:1 onto four of the JD's six pillars without naming them.

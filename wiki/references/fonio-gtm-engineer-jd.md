## What this role actually is

Weighted reading of the JD:

| Pillar | Weight | What it actually is |
|---|---|---|
| Lead-gen workflow construction | ~30% | Build the ICP → enrichment → outbound pipeline with Clay/Apollo/HubSpot/Instantly/Smartlead/PhantomBuster. Tool-stack is named explicitly; the work is plumbing those tools into one production system, not picking one. |
| Lead research + enrichment via APIs | ~20% | Pull from multiple data sources and APIs (people, firmographic, intent), normalize, qualify. The "you can read an API doc without panicking" bar is the load-bearing technical filter. |
| Outbound campaign development | ~20% | Targeting + messaging + deliverability, co-owned with the founding team. Deliverability is named explicitly — this is the operations-not-creative side of outbound. |
| Sales & marketing automation with AI | ~15% | AI-native automation of pipeline plumbing across the SaaS stack. Effectively: "use LLMs anywhere they replace 30 minutes of manual research per lead." |
| Funnel / conversion / campaign analytics | ~10% | Continuous optimization based on data; implies SQL or Clay-native analytics, dashboarding, basic stats on cohort performance. |
| Product feedback loop | ~5% | Direct collaboration with Product & Engineering to push GTM learnings back into the product. Lightweight responsibility; more "channel exists" than "named workstream." |

Effective role: **operator-engineer plumbing the outbound machine** at a hypergrowth German-speaking AI-telephony scaleup. The role lives on the GTM org, but the day-to-day is closer to systems-engineering for a sales/marketing stack than to traditional SDR/AE/marketer work. No P&L ownership, no managerial scope, no direct revenue quota named in the JD ^[inferred].

The "first dedicated GTM Engineer" framing is not explicit in the JD, but the company size, the breadth of the JD's scope, and the direct-collaboration-with-founders phrasing make it likely that this is **either the founding GTM Engineer hire or one of a very small team** ^[inferred]. The 90-minute "GTM Case" technical-interview format reinforces that the technical-build portion is the load-bearing screen, not the sales-pitch portion.

## Application angle if pursued

Differentiator vs typical GTM Engineer applicants (mostly RevOps analysts moving up the stack, or junior SDRs moving sideways into ops):

1. **Three shipped AI sales tools, not framework-only theory.** [[skills/ai-powered-sales-tooling]] is exactly the artifact this role exists to build. Lead with one concrete tool, ideally the ABM data layer (Clay-native, multi-source enrichment, exactly the "research + enrichment + qualification via APIs" pillar of the JD). Most applicants describe Clay workflows; Mark has shipped raw-LLM-API automations that complement the Clay stack and demonstrably outperform it for specific tasks. This is "kein Senior Engineer, aber..." in shipped form.
2. **Founding-GTM scale claim.** $50K → $1M+ ARR at Stackgini is the kind of compounding-systems claim a GTM Engineer seat values higher than a closing-quota claim. Lead with the systems built, not the deals closed.
3. **Cross-pillar founding ownership.** [[entities/mark-vavulov]] — Mark has done outbound, ABM, content, channel, customer success, fundraising-support, and product feedback simultaneously for ~2.5 years. The JD's "direkte Zusammenarbeit mit Product & Engineering, um GTM-Learnings zurück ins Produkt einfließen zu lassen" pillar matches a real reps-pattern Mark has, not a hypothetical.
4. **AI-first vocabulary, not demand-gen lexicon.** Mirror fonio's voice: "Systeme bauen", "Hypergrowth", "shipst", "10x". Avoid funnel-jargon ("MQL", "BANT", "demand capture") — wrong language for a GTM Engineer seat at an AI-native scaleup.
5. **Why-fonio narrative**: lean on (a) voice-AI as the next-generation SMB CRM surface (genuine technological inflection — AI telephony is currently in its first commercial wave, not a saturated category), (b) DACH-native + Stackgini DACH-anchor (zero ramp on the buyer's native language and business culture), (c) tool-stack overlap (no ramp on the GTM stack itself; immediate productivity). Avoid generic "I want to work at an AI company" — fonio is a vertical AI scaleup, not a horizontal AI lab; the why-fonio is the *DACH SMB voice-AI* thesis, not the AI thesis.

**Counter-signals to weigh before applying:**
- The progression question: is the **month-13 seat** at fonio a recognizable next step (founding GTM Eng, then Head of GTM Engineering at €10M+ ARR scale; or sideways into Growth Eng at a larger company) or does the seat stay a permanent IC plumber? Mark's filter ([[synthesis/mark-progression-over-direction]]) makes this load-bearing. Needs a screen-level answer.
- SMB-volume outbound mechanics are operationally distinct from intra-IT enterprise outbound. The mechanic-translation cost is real and would be visible in the first 90 days.
- "First fully automated CRM" framing — if this is a serious roadmap, the seat is closer to **product-engineering with a GTM lens** than to GTM-Eng. If it is a recruiter line, ignore. Surface in the screen.
- Equity disclosure gap — at €5M ARR + 30% MoM, the equity component is where the asymmetric upside lives. Cash band alone does not tell you whether this is a take-the-job-for-equity scaleup or a market-rate hire.
